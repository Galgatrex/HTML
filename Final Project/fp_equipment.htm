In our line of work, BMETs see millions of dollars worth of equipment in a single hospital. This amounts to thousands of different items. Needless to say, there is a lot to learn!

There are many different sections of a hospital, all with unique pieces of equipment. Some more information about the distinctions between these can be found here.

Each piece of equipment has a different check out, and each checkout can take between a few minutes to entire days to complete.

When you account for the thousdands of different items that need to be tested, this task can become quite an undertaking.

The items shown below are equipment items that are used to transport patients from wherever they are picked up back to a hospital that is capable of helping them recover.

Table of Equipment
Name Description Picture
Suction Unit This unit is responsible for sucking up all excess fluid that might be in a serious wound. It is often used in surgery or during transport of a critical patient.
Infusion Pump You have probably seen these around a standard hospital room. They are used for pumping fluids back into a patient. This can be water, blood, or medication.
Defibrillator This is a popularly used TV item. It is used for getting a patient's heart beating after it has stopped, or when it is beating irregularly.
This particular item also doubles as a vital signs monitor, which can perform simple tasks such as: taking blood pressure, temperature, or an ECG.
Ventilator This is used to keep a patient breathing after their body no longer has the strength to do so for them. It will continually provide air to a patient's lungs at a set interval and lung capacity.
