Overview:

I have been a Biomedical Equipment Technician (BMET) for 7 years. I have had the opportunity to deploy twice as BMET, and I have worked at four different locations. I have worked on thousands of different equipment items, and I have been exposed to hundreds of different types of equipment.

During my career I have come across many people who have never heard of BMETs, or may only have minimal information. This page is dedicated to the education of family, friends, and all others who wish to know a little bit more about what BMETs are and what they do on a daily basis.

General Description:

BMETs install, inspect, repair, and modify biomedical equipment and support systems. We perform pre-purchase evaluations of medical devices and advise on operational theory, underlying physiological principles, and safe clinical applications of biomedical equipment. Implements organizational maintenance support for all medical devices used within the medical treatment facility (MTF), medical research laboratories, air transportable hospitals and clinics, and contingency hospitals. This is an excerpt from the usmilitary.about.com description. The rest of the description can be found here.

In fewer words, we essentially install, fix, and maintain all of the different types of equipment throughout hospitals. All equipment should be inspected by an authority such as a trained BMET to prevent harm to patients. Most items are inspected annually, but many higher risk items are inspected more frequently. This is all done to ensure that no harm comes to any patient due to unsafe or improperly functioning medical equipment.
